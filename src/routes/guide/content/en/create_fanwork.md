# Fanwork Guidelines

{{PUBLIC_BRAND_NAME}} does not categorically ban fanart, homage, parody, or other derivative works.
However, whether such content can remain public on the Service is judged under the standards below.

## ✅ Core Principles

1. **Original license comes first**
   * The license or usage terms from the original creator, distributor, or asset marketplace take priority over this guide.
   * You must directly confirm the allowed scope for assets such as Live2D models, VRM files, illustrations, voices, and logos.

2. **Make it clearly unofficial**
   * You must not present derivative content as an official sequel, official collaboration, or official account.
   * We recommend labels such as `fanmade`, `unofficial`, `homage`, or `parody` in the title or description.

3. **No raw asset redistribution**
   * Re-uploading or bundling original images, model files, voice files, logos, or UI resources with little to no transformation may be prohibited.
   * Fanwork must be transformative. It cannot simply function as a rehost of the original material.

4. **Operational action may follow disputes**
   * If we receive a rights-holder report, a platform inquiry, or a reasonable infringement claim, we may temporarily hide, request edits to, or remove the content.
   * We may also request proof of license, permission, or source attribution from the creator.

> [!NOTE]
> For baseline notices related to uploaded Live2D or VRM assets, also review [Licenses & Credits](/licenses).

## Examples Generally Allowed

* Fan characters, homage concepts, and parody personas built with original interpretation
* Content with a title, thumbnail, and description that clearly distinguish it from the source work
* Non-commercial usage, or usage that stays within the original creator's published fanwork rules
* Descriptions that disclose the source IP or inspiration so users can easily understand it is unofficial

## Prohibited or Restricted Cases

1. **Violation of the original creator's rules**
   * When the original IP owner prohibits derivative works, commercial use, or platform uploads

2. **Impersonation or misleading presentation**
   * Making the content appear to be an official series, official account, or licensed product
   * Claiming approval, partnership, or endorsement that does not actually exist

3. **Re-uploading original assets**
   * Re-distributing original illustrations, Live2D files, VRM files, voice assets, music, or logos with minimal change

4. **Harmful distortion or unlawful use**
   * Using the work to defame a real person, a specific group, or the original creator
   * Combining it with illegal, hateful, or child-safety violating content

5. **Refusing rights-dispute handling**
   * Repeatedly ignoring requests for explanation, edits, or takedown
   * Falsely claiming ownership or permission

## Recommended Labeling

* Add one of the following in the title or description: `fanmade`, `unofficial`, `homage`, `parody`
* When possible, name the source work, rights holder, or reference material in the description
* Before monetization, ads, or external support links, confirm that the original IP allows commercial use

## Operating Standard

* Public visibility on the Service may be judged by both automated detection and manual review
* Rights complaints may result in temporary hiding without prior notice
* Repeated violations may lead to visibility limits, monetization restrictions, upload limits, or account sanctions

> [!IMPORTANT]
> This guide is the Service's publication standard, not a replacement for the actual legal scope of use. Original licenses, applicable law, and separate contracts take priority.
